Communication Technologies

A reference of wired and wireless technologies, network types, and topologies used in modern data communication.

Network Topologies

A network topology describes how devices are physically or logically connected to each other.

🚌 Bus

All devices share a single backbone cable. Simple and cheap, but a single break brings down the whole network. Largely obsolete.

⭐ Star

All devices connect to a central switch or hub. Failure of one device doesn't affect others. The most common LAN topology today.

🔄 Ring

Devices form a closed loop. Data travels in one direction around the ring. A single device failure can disrupt the whole network.

🕸️ Mesh

Every device connects to every other device. Extremely fault-tolerant but very expensive. Used in critical infrastructure and WANs.

🌳 Tree

A hierarchy of star-topology networks connected to a central root. Scalable and used in large corporate and campus networks.

🔀 Hybrid

A combination of two or more topologies. Most real-world networks are hybrid — e.g. a star-bus or star-ring arrangement.
